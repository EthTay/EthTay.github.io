Dr Ethan D. Taylor

Postdoctoral Research Associate
University of Surrey

Hello! Have you ever wondered what the universe is made of? What keeps galaxies held together? Or even what the oldest or smallest stellar systems are in the universe? If so you are in the right place!

I study Dwarf Galaxies, the universes oldest and most dark matter dominated galaxies. These galaxies are so small that they are extremely sensitive to Stellar Feedback from the stars they play host to making them excellent places to study Dark Matter. At the same time, however, Globular Clusters are forming completely free of the mysterious Dark Matter. I study both of these objects using high resolution Computational Simulations apart of the EDGE Simulation suite that are able to form both Globular Clusters and Dwarf Galaxies in the same simulation!
